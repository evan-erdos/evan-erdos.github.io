About Me

Hi. I'm a student at Carnegie Mellon, and a bunch of other things. I like 3D modeling, game development, David Bowie, things made of titanium, architecture, playing instruments with strings on them, programming in C, snowboarding & windsurfing (sports where the goal is to stay out of water, as opposed to swimming), text adventures, the harmony parts in rock ballads, pen-and-ink drawings, graphic design, digital sculpture (which is basically the first one, but squishier), and rocket science. I'm most adept with 3D modeling, and I don't actually know how to do rocket science. Below are some of the things that I do.

Game Development

These projects are my proudest works. They exist at the confluence of all these other media (restrain from throwing things across rooms because I used that word), and the sum is far greater than the parts. While this whole process is considered "Game Development", and while I do make games with it, I find that there's more potential in this medium. I also think there's a trivial, and moreover justified stigma about "games", which evokes a notion of wasting time on your coffee break or playing a colorful puzzle thing on your iPhone on the bus to accumulate "points". I want to distance myself from that. Hopefully, my work will do that for me.

3D Modeling

I do the lion's share of my 3D modeling in either AutoCAD or ZBrush (I have an A-to-Z understanding of both of them, really). They're on opposite ends of a spectrum, and I find that they complement each other very nicely, in terms of the kinds of geometry they let me make. AutoCAD lets me type numbers in and calculate involute curves on gears and whatnot with AutoLISP, and that leads to very rigid, very precise geometry. ZBrush is exactly the opposite. There's no measuring involved, and most of the workflow consists of pushing thousands of polygons around to make more organic models.

Recordings

I've got a nasty habit of getting distracted from whatever I am supposed to be doing, and then wandering off to do something else. "Something Else (tm)" typically involves one of the categories on this page, and this is no exception. Sometimes, I'll gravitate towards the nearest piano like some sort of musical homing pigeon, and I'll just start accosting it. I'll sit down, and start screaming at it until people ask me to leave. Sometimes my singing has nuance and tenderness, but it more often resembles lambasting. I like David Bowie, Paul McCartney (and those other guys who followed him around for awhile in the 60s), Stevie Wonder, Bob Dylan, and the Velvet Underground. I like to pretend to be Mick Jagger, which the confining nature of pianos doesn't always allow for, but I make do. Brian Wilson and the Beach Boys also constitute a large part of my repertoire. Here are some extremely raw, perfectly uncut recordings of my better sessions. I typically play 3-5 hours at a time (due more to inattention and a lack of time sense rather than any actual dedication to the art), and there's a lot of mistakes and downtime between song selection, but I've isolated the best parts in individual files.

Text Highlighting

I have a really cool syntax highlighter that I wrote for Sublime Text. It works for anything that could be mistaken for C, and borders on being gaudy and not tasteful. Class names and accessors have gradients, parentheses both change colors as they nest, and are also somewhat context-sensitive, so the parentheses for an if statement show up blue, while others remain white. It's on my GitHub page.
